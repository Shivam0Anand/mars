MARS

Mars is a future themed campground hosting learning communities in the California Desert January - March 2020.

Amenities

Mars has a large open camping area for tents, vans, small trailers, hexayurts, and other future themed experimental living units. Base camp has wifi and electricity to charge your devices. It has screening lounges, closed conference rooms, and quiet working areas. There are toilets and showers around. As we’re in the desert we ask people to be very frugal with their water consumption. Water and tank pumping services are available to trailers and larger camps. There are no blacktop roads or concrete pads. The dirt road is ok to drive on except when its wet. The rain is infrequent.
Bombay Beach has a small market at quaint bar & restaurant. Larger stores like Walmart and Home Depot are an hour away in Palm Springs or El Centro.

Activities

Bombay Beach is a mile away and has an active artist community largely oriented around the Bombay Beach Biennale Our sister space Brahma.ai is also in Bombay Beach and has a lounge and work space Mars campers can visit.
What happens on Mars specifically will largely be up to you and the other campers there. We hope that people organize regular study groups, and video screenings, give workshops, host talks and generally help make the community a vital interesting place to be. We maintain a community calendar and a variety of digital communication tools. If you have an idea for an event just announce it to the group, and let us know if you need to reserve a space for it or need support in some other way.

Camps

Camps drive the Mars experience and we push everyone on Mars to participate in one of the camps. Camps collectively provide additional infrastructure support to their members, organize structured learning activities, and host open social gatherings. Mars provides additional services like water, solar, internet and tank pumping to camps.

Culture

We strive to adhere to Burningman’s 10 principles.

Burningman has been an amazing experience for many people living in the desert, taking care of their own needs, expressing themselves fully and giving generously. The Mars Project attempts to maintain this type of vitality from January through March with a focus on technology and online learning.
